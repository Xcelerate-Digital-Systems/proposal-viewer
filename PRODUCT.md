# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Marketing and creative agency teams (account managers, creative leads, strategists, owners) who build and present deliverables to clients. They work in a fast-paced environment where first impressions matter, proposals need to close fast, and creative revisions need to be tracked without drowning in email threads. Their clients are non-technical decision-makers who open links on desktop and expect things to just work.

## Product Purpose

AgencyViz consolidates every client-facing touchpoint into one workspace: proposals, quotes, documents, campaign review boards, funnel plans, and integrations. It exists so agencies stop stitching together five separate tools and start presenting everything through a single, branded experience. Success is when an agency's client opens one link, engages with the content, and signs off without friction.

## Brand Personality

Premium, minimal, precise. AgencyViz is a professional instrument, not a toy. It speaks with the confidence of a tool that knows what it does and doesn't oversell. Copy is direct, interfaces are clean, and every element earns its place. No whimsy, no fluff, no hand-holding past the first session.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS dashboards**: white panels, blue accents, Bootstrap/Material defaults. HubSpot, Monday.com, cookie-cutter admin templates. AgencyViz should feel authored, not assembled from a kit.
- **Overly playful tools**: illustration-heavy, rounded, cartoon-ish UI. Canva, Mailchimp, Asana. Agencies sell professionalism to their clients; the tool they use to do it should reflect that.
- **Dense enterprise UIs**: cramped tables, tiny text, no breathing room. Salesforce, Jira, old-school CRM energy. Information density is fine where it serves the task, but never at the cost of legibility or composure.

## Design Principles

1. **Disappear into the work.** The tool's job is to make the agency's deliverables look good, not to show off its own UI. Chrome recedes; content commands attention.
2. **Agency-grade polish.** If agencies sell design quality to their clients, the tool they build in must meet that standard. Every surface should feel like something an agency would be proud to show a client.
3. **Earned density.** Show what helps. A campaign board with 30 assets needs tight rows. An empty proposal editor needs breathing room. Density follows the task, not a global setting.
4. **One workspace, not many tools.** Pitch, Campaigns, Funnels, Integrations all share the same visual language. Moving between sections should feel like switching tabs, not switching apps.
5. **Precision over decoration.** Alignment, spacing, and type hierarchy do the work. No decorative flourishes, no gratuitous motion, no elements that exist only to fill space.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance: 4.5:1 contrast for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components, full keyboard navigation, screen reader support. Reduced-motion alternatives for animations are already implemented in globals.css. Color is never the sole indicator of state; always pair with shape, icon, or label.
