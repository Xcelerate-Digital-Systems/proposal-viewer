## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment: Clean.** This viewer would not trigger an "AI made that" reaction. The editorial quote layout, three-tier branding cascade, and restrained component vocabulary read as authored design, not template assembly. Two minor flags:

- **Zero-padded step numbers** (`01`, `02`, `03`) in Next Steps sections (QuoteSinglePageView:863, ViewerPageContent:186) are on the ban list. The format adds no information and reads as template-ish.
- **Uppercase tracked labels** (`text-2xs tracking-[0.22em] uppercase`) are used pervasively across the quote — ~12 instances for section headers, eyebrows, and metadata. Each instance is justified individually, but the accumulated pattern starts to feel like a system tic rather than deliberate hierarchy.

Neither rises to slop-level. The viewer passes the product slop test: a user fluent in PandaDoc, Qwilr, or HoneyBook would trust this interface.

**Deterministic scan: 1 finding, false positive.** The detector flagged a `borderLeft: 3px solid` in TextPage.tsx:275 — this is inside a blockquote renderer in the rich-text pipeline, not a card accent stripe. It's the conventional blockquote treatment. No real violations found across 18 components.

## Overall Impression

The viewer is the strongest surface in the AgencyViz codebase. The three-tier branding cascade, the editorial quote layout, and the mobile-differentiated proposal/quote modes are genuinely premium work. The cover page experience, the Investment card echoing the header gradient, and the expiry urgency system with 5 distinct states all demonstrate real design thinking.

The biggest gap is at the moment of truth: when a client is about to commit to a five-figure quote, the decision area shows no amount recap, no confirmation step, and silently swallows errors. This is where competing tools (PandaDoc, HoneyBook) invest heavily, and where AgencyViz currently relies on hope that the network holds and the client remembers what they read.

## What's Working

**1. The three-tier branding cascade is genuinely excellent.** Every visual element — cover, header, section labels, decision panel, hairlines — pulls colors and fonts through `proposal → branding → fallback`. Agencies get real brand control without configuring individual elements. The `useBrandPalette` hook generating 17 semantic tokens from 5 inputs via OKLCH is architecturally sound.

**2. The quote viewer's editorial layout is premium.** The gradient header band, typographic hierarchy, Investment card reusing the header gradient, trust badges, and the 5-state expiry system create a document that competes with dedicated proposal tools. The Section/Hairline rhythm and generous whitespace (`py-10`, `px-8 sm:px-14`) feel considered, not default.

**3. Mobile is thoughtfully differentiated.** Quotes use single-scroll (no sidebar needed); proposals get hamburger menu + slide-over sidebar + bottom-sheet comments. The `h-dvh` viewport handling, `pb-safe` on mobile comment forms, and responsive padding are correct implementation details that many viewers get wrong.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] No amount recap or confirmation before accepting a quote/proposal

The ProposalDecisionPanel submits immediately on button click. For a client accepting a $50,000+ quote, the total amount is not visible in the decision area — it may be hundreds of pixels above (quote) or several pages back (proposal). The confirmation says "Thanks — we'll be in touch shortly" but does not confirm what was accepted.

**Why it matters**: This is the most consequential action in the entire product. Competing tools show the amount in the signature area. Agencies need legal clarity that the client saw the number before accepting.

**Fix**: Add a summary line above the submit button showing the title and total amount. Consider a 2-step confirmation: click "Accept" → button text changes to "Confirm Acceptance of $X,XXX" → second click submits.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable harden view/[token]`

### [P1] No error feedback on decision submission failure

If `onAccept`/`onDecline`/`onRequestRevision` network calls fail, the `finally` block (ProposalDecisionPanel:168) resets `submitting` but the user sees nothing. On a flaky mobile connection, a client could try to accept, see it "hang," then return to the pending state with no explanation. They might assume it worked and leave.

**Why it matters**: Silent failure on the highest-stakes form in the product. A client who thinks they accepted but didn't creates confusion for both parties.

**Fix**: Add `[submissionError, setSubmissionError]` state. In the catch block, show "Something went wrong — please try again" below the submit button. Apply the same pattern to composite download and share actions.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable harden view/[token]`

### [P2] Expired quotes can still be accepted

`computeExpiryState` (QuoteSinglePageView:137) correctly calculates and displays expiry banners, but nothing prevents submission. The decision panel renders and functions normally regardless of expiry state.

**Why it matters**: An agency sends a quote valid for 14 days. 30 days later, the client opens the link and accepts at a price the agency may no longer honor. Legal ambiguity.

**Fix**: Pass expiry state to ProposalDecisionPanel. When expired, disable Accept (greyed button + "This quote has expired — contact [company] for an updated quote"). Keep Decline and Request Changes functional.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable harden view/[token]`

### [P3] Share button gives no clipboard feedback

When clicking Share on the FloatingToolbar (line 79-85), on desktop the URL is silently copied to clipboard. No visual confirmation. The user has no idea if it worked.

**Why it matters**: "Copy to clipboard" without feedback is a known anti-pattern. Users will either retry or manually copy from the address bar.

**Fix**: After `navigator.clipboard.writeText()` succeeds, briefly swap the Share2 icon to a Check icon for 2 seconds, or show a small "Copied!" tooltip.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable polish view/[token]`

### [P3] Not-found page offers no recovery path

The not-found state (page.tsx:83-94) shows "Proposal Not Found — This link may have expired or been removed" with no contact info, no way to request a new link, and no agency branding. It also says "Proposal" even for quote tokens.

**Why it matters**: This is the worst-case ending for a client clicking a link. It should at minimum show the agency's name/contact if recoverable from the token lookup, and not assume the entity type.

**Fix**: If any partial data is available from the token lookup (company name, contact email), show it. Change "Proposal" to a generic "Document" or derive from entity_type if known.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable clarify view/[token]`

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (First-Timer)**: The cover page CTA defaults to "START READING PROPOSAL" even for quotes (CoverPage:154). Jordan opens a quote link and sees "START READING PROPOSAL" — confusing. The not-found page similarly says "Proposal Not Found" regardless of entity type. The decision form's "Request Changes" tab has no explanation of what happens next — Jordan may hesitate because they don't know if it cancels the quote or just sends a message.

**Casey (Mobile User)**: The mobile header ChevronLeft/Right buttons are `p-1.5` with 18px icons — roughly 30x30px touch targets, well below Apple's 44x44px minimum. The floating "Download PDF" button at `bottom-5 right-5` may overlap the iPhone home indicator. The signature capture canvas is `h-28` (112px) — very small for drawing a signature on a phone.

**Riley (Stress Tester)**: A quote with zero line items still renders the Investment card showing "$0.00" — looks broken. A proposal with 100+ pages would render all sidebar items without virtualization. Extreme branding values (white accent on white body bg) have no contrast check — the floating toolbar could become invisible.

## Minor Observations

- `withAlpha` is duplicated in 3 files (ViewerPageContent:56, QuoteSinglePageView:60, ProposalDecisionPanel:88) — should be a shared utility.
- FloatingToolbar uses `onMouseEnter`/`onMouseLeave` JS for hover states instead of CSS `:hover` — unusual and potentially sluggish.
- `handlePrint` function in FloatingToolbar is dead code (defined but never rendered in JSX).
- CommentsPanel duplicates its entire render between desktop (lines 177-227) and mobile (lines 230-281) — only layout wrappers differ.
- Cover page CTA default "START READING PROPOSAL" should be entity-type-aware.
- Multiple `eslint-disable` for `@typescript-eslint/no-explicit-any` suggest the Proposal type needs broadening.

## Questions to Consider

- **Should the viewer pre-fill the signer name?** The proposal is sent to a specific `client_name`. The decision panel asks them to type their full name from scratch. Pre-filling would reduce friction on the highest-stakes form.
- **Is single-scroll the right pattern for a 30-item quote?** For complex trade quotes with detailed scope, photos, attachments, and terms, the decision form could be 4000+ pixels below the fold. Should long quotes get a sticky "Accept" FAB or a sidebar TOC?
- **Does the viewer need graceful degradation for failed image loads?** The viewer fetches signed URLs for cover images, project photos, and attachments — all silently swallowed on failure. On job sites with poor connectivity, the viewer degrades to text-only with no indication that images are missing.
