## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment**: Minor tells. Four tiny-uppercase-tracked eyebrows above every section (`Founding-member pricing`, `What's included`, `Compare`, `FAQ`) use the identical `text-xs font-semibold uppercase tracking-wider text-teal` pattern. This is the SaaS landing page scaffold that every AI generator produces. The 1px gradient stripe on the plan card (`h-1 bg-gradient-to-r from-teal/40 via-teal to-teal/40`) is a known template tell. The 6 identical white feature cards in a 3×2 grid match the "identical card grid" ban. The overall page structure (hero → single plan card → feature grid → comparison table → FAQ → dark CTA) follows the Stripe/Framer template cadence precisely. No glassmorphism, no gradient text, no cream background, no numbered section markers — so not egregious, but the eyebrow repetition and feature grid sameness are the strongest tells.

**Deterministic scan**: 0 findings across all 8 files scanned (page + 7 marketing components). The automated detector rules did not flag structural anti-patterns. The LLM caught the eyebrow repetition and card grid sameness that fall below the detector's rule thresholds.

## Overall Impression

A competent, clean pricing page that gets the fundamentals right: single plan eliminates tier-comparison paralysis, the billing toggle is genuinely polished, and the comparison table is effective persuasion. Three structural problems hold it back: (1) interactive components (FAQ accordion, billing toggle) lack ARIA attributes and focus styles — a shipping blocker for accessibility; (2) the feature grid creates a dead zone in the middle of the page where cognitive load spikes and engagement drops; (3) `text-muted` (#8A8A8A) fails WCAG AA contrast at 3.54:1 on white, affecting FAQ answers, plan card subtext, and hero subtitle. The strongest moment is the comparison table. The weakest is the 37-item feature dump.

## What's Working

1. **The comparison table is genuinely effective persuasion.** Red competitor prices create loss aversion, then the teal AgencyViz row creates contrast and relief. The specific tool-to-competitor mapping (Proposify, Filestage, Funnelytics) grounds the comparison in reality rather than abstract "save money" claims. This is the best section on the page.

2. **The billing toggle is polished.** The framer-motion `layoutId` sliding pill with NumberFlow animated price is a delightful micro-interaction that respects `prefers-reduced-motion` via `useReducedMotion`. The toggle is the single best UX element on the page.

3. **Single-plan pricing is the right call.** No analysis paralysis from tier comparison. The "founding member" framing with the future $79/mo anchor price is effective price anchoring without being manipulative.

## Priority Issues

### [P0] FAQ accordion and billing toggle lack ARIA attributes and focus styles

**Why it matters**: Both interactive components — the FAQ accordion and the billing period toggle — have zero ARIA attributes. The FAQ buttons have no `aria-expanded`, no `aria-controls`, no `id` linkage between trigger and panel. The toggle has no `role="radiogroup"`, no `aria-checked`, no `aria-label`. Neither has `focus-visible` ring styles. Screen readers cannot determine expand/collapse state or toggle selection. Keyboard-only users cannot see what's focused. This is a WCAG 2.1 AA failure (4.1.2 Name, Role, Value) across both components.

**Fix**: FAQ: add `aria-expanded={isOpen}`, `aria-controls={panelId}`, `id={buttonId}` to buttons; add `role="region"`, `aria-labelledby={buttonId}`, `id={panelId}` to panels; add `focus-visible:ring-2 focus-visible:ring-primary/40`. Toggle: add `role="radiogroup"` and `aria-label="Billing period"` to container; add `role="radio"`, `aria-checked={active}`, and focus-visible ring to each button.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable harden app/pricing`

### [P1] Custom scroll container hijacks native browser scroll

**Why it matters**: The outer `<div className="h-[100dvh] overflow-y-auto">` creates a custom scroll container that breaks: (1) browser find-in-page (Cmd+F) in some browsers, (2) scroll position restoration on back-navigation, (3) mobile Safari URL bar auto-hide behavior, (4) anchor link smooth scrolling. The `SiteHeader` `useScrolled` hook manually walks the DOM to find this container, which is fragile.

**Fix**: Remove `h-[100dvh] overflow-y-auto` from the outer div. Use natural document flow. If sticky header behavior needs it, use `position: sticky` on the body's natural scroll.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable audit app/pricing`

### [P1] No `<main>` landmark element

**Why it matters**: The page has `<header>`, `<section>`, `<footer>` but no `<main>` landmark. Screen readers use landmarks for page navigation. Without `<main>`, users relying on landmark navigation cannot skip to the primary content. WCAG 2.1 failure (1.3.1 Info and Relationships).

**Fix**: Wrap the content sections (between SiteHeader and SiteFooter) in a `<main>` element.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable harden app/pricing`

### [P1] Feature grid presents 37+ items across 6 groups with no progressive disclosure

**Why it matters**: The "Everything included" section dumps all features simultaneously. Six groups exceeds the 4-item chunking guideline. Each group has 5–8 items, all visible at once. On mobile, this becomes a very long scroll of near-identical white cards. Cognitive load spikes here and emotional engagement drops. Users are unlikely to read past the second card. The information density works against the sale — it creates the feeling of complexity rather than completeness.

**Fix**: Collapse into an accordion or tabs showing 3–4 top groups by default, with a "See all features" expansion. Or reduce to a curated highlight list (8–10 best features) with the full breakdown available on expand. Group the 6 categories into 3 super-groups (Build, Review, Connect) to stay within chunking limits.

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable layout app/pricing`

### [P2] `text-muted` (#8A8A8A) fails WCAG AA contrast on white

**Why it matters**: `text-muted` at #8A8A8A on white yields 3.54:1 contrast — below the 4.5:1 requirement for body text. Affects FAQ answer text (`text-sm text-muted`, 14px), plan card subtext, comparison table parentheticals, and billing period label. The hero subtitle at `text-lg text-muted` (18px) qualifies as large text (3:1 threshold) and borderline passes, but smaller instances fail.

**Fix**: Replace `text-muted` with `text-prose` (#4B5563) for body-sized text, or darken the muted token to at least #767676 (which hits 4.5:1 on white).

**Suggested command**: `/impeccable audit app/pricing`

## Minor Observations

- `stackTotal` on line 122 (`STACK_COMPARISON.reduce((_, __) => 0, 0)`) always returns 0 — broken dead code. The variable is never rendered. Remove it.
- `import Link from 'next/link'` on line 2 is unused — all links are raw `<a>` tags. Dead import.
- Plan card CTA and final CTA use different button styles (`bg-primary text-white` vs `bg-white text-teal`) for the same action. Visual inconsistency.
- Both CTAs are raw `<a>` tags instead of the project's `<Button>` primitive or `buttonClasses` utility.
- SiteFooter uses `bg-teal` as full background immediately after the dark surface CTA section — two consecutive dark/colored sections create visual heaviness at the page bottom.
- The `ScrollReveal` IntersectionObserver uses viewport as root, but the page uses a custom scroll container — animations may trigger at incorrect positions.
- Feature item "Meta + GoHighLevel to Looker Studio" is highly specific jargon. Lead with the benefit: "Pipe ad data into your dashboards."
- Four identical eyebrow labels (`text-xs font-semibold uppercase tracking-wider text-teal`) above every section. "Compare" above "Add up what you're paying now" is redundant. "FAQ" above "Pricing questions, answered" is redundant. Remove at least two.

## Questions to Consider

- Where is the social proof? No testimonials, no customer count, no agency logos. For a B2B SaaS asking for a credit card on a 7-day trial, zero trust signals is a conversion problem. Would you give your card to a product with no evidence anyone else uses it?
- The value proposition is "One plan. Everything in." — simplicity. But the page then dumps 37 feature bullet points. Is this section selling to the visitor, or reassuring the founder?
- The comparison table and FAQ are the strongest sections. Both are below the feature grid dead zone. Would the page convert better with the comparison table promoted above the feature list?
- Two CTA moments (plan card, final section) but zero mid-page CTAs. The feature grid and comparison table — where buying intent peaks — have no call to action. Is the visitor expected to scroll back up?
